Covering Letter

Subject: Celebrating 25 Years of Connecting India to the World

Tata Communications began in 1986 as Videsh Sanchar Nigam Limited, when international calls were a luxury and the internet was still a distant dream. You were India's gateway to the global conversation — literally the only way most Indians could speak to family abroad or businesses could reach international markets.

Twenty-five years ago, in 1999, you transformed into Tata Communications. The world was on the cusp of the dot-com boom. Y2K fears dominated headlines. Most Indians had never sent an email. You saw what others couldn't: that connectivity wasn't just about phone lines anymore. It was about building the invisible infrastructure that would power India's digital future.

Today, you carry 30% of the world's internet traffic. Your submarine cables span oceans. Your network connects 200+ countries. You've evolved from a state-owned telecom operator to a digital ecosystem enabler, powering everything from Formula 1 broadcasts to Microsoft's cloud services. That's not just growth — that's transformation at the speed of light.

Here's the challenge with milestone campaigns: everyone defaults to looking backward. "Remember when we started with rotary phones?" The result is usually a nostalgic video montage that makes your brand feel old rather than pioneering.

We see this differently. Your 25th anniversary isn't about celebrating the past — it's about declaring what the next 25 years will bring. We helped Bajaj Auto reframe their centenary not as "100 years old" but as "100 years ahead." The campaign drove their highest brand consideration scores in a decade and delivered a 23% increase in premium model sales.

The idea: "25 Years Forward." Instead of looking back at dial-up modems and fax machines, we show the world you're building — 6G networks, quantum-secure communications, AI-powered infrastructure. We make your anniversary a statement of intent, not a retirement party.

Our approach spans four waves across the year. Wave one launches with a prediction campaign — "The World in 2049" — positioning you as the architect of tomorrow's connectivity. Wave two brings these predictions to life through immersive experiences at key industry events. Wave three amplifies your leadership voices as futurists, not historians. Wave four closes with a global summit where you announce the next frontier you're conquering.

Every touchpoint reinforces the same message: while others connect the present, Tata Communications is building the future.

Would Wednesday, January 15th at 2pm work for a 45-minute strategy walkthrough? We can present via your own network — seems fitting.

Arjun
